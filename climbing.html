Mindset

Our mindset plays a crucial role in determining our success in any endeavor. It encompasses our attitudes, beliefs, and perspectives that shape how we approach and navigate challenges, setbacks, and opportunities. A positive and growth-oriented mindset can be a powerful tool for achieving success, while a negative or fixed mindset can impede progress and limit our potential.

It is through failure that we better understand how grow.

Physicality

The physical nature of climbing is of utmost importance as it directly affects our ability to navigate and conquer challenging routes or problems. Climbing requires a unique combination of physical strength, balance, and coordination.

During our sessions we'll help you develop your body positioning, foot work, stamina, and balance by introducing you to fun on-the-wall drills. Topics that we'll cover include:

  • Foot work
  • Static vs Dynamic motion
  • Body positioning
  • The importance of stamina

Get in touch with me



When I first started climbing I was a shameless try-hard. At the time I was convinced that in order to improve my climbing I needed a stronger upper body. This approach got me to about the V7 range before my progress began to stone wall. I spent months trying to break the V7 range and the moment I was starting to see an inkling of success ... Covid hit. All of the progress I had built up came crumbling down.

Like many others I found sitting at home to be a drain on my mental state. The monotony of working from home, the lack of social interactions, and having to sit still only fanned the flames of frustration. But rather than let that consume me I turned to action: what could I do to try to retain my climbing skills? It started simple: create a work out plan.

But I have one rule for myself: I do not train in the classical sense. This means no hangboarding, no pull-ups, push-ups, planks, etc. That's my self imposed challenge and as long as I continue to see progress (V9s) then I'll continue onwards.
